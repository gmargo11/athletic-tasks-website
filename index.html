Bridging the Sim-to-Real Gap for Athletic Loco-Manipulation

Nolan Fey     Gabriel B. Margolis     Martin Peticco     Pulkit Agrawal



Abstract

Training controllers via reinforcement learning (RL) in simulation has emerged as a powerful approach for synthesizing robust and agile robotic behaviors evaluated in reality. In our work, we present a two-stage training pipeline for whole-body athletic loco-manipulation. First, a whole-body controller (WBC) is pre-trained to track reference trajectories; then it is fine-tuned with task-specific rewards to learn dynamic behaviors. In addition, we introduce an unsupervised actuator net (UAN) that minimizes the sim-to-real gap by learning corrective torques from real hardware data without relying on direct torque sensing. We demonstrate athletic tasks – including ball throwing, dumbbell snatch, and sled pulling – on a quadruped robot with an arm. Our results show that combining WBC pre-training with UAN-based sim-to-real calibration enables effective transfer of agile whole-body control policies from simulation to reality.
